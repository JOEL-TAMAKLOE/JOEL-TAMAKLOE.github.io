Reviving Ride Demand Through Data-Driven Field Strategy: A Yango Experience

By Joel Tamakloe · Published: January 2026 · 7 min read
Data Analysis Business Strategy Field Operations Marketplace Growth Ride-Hailing

Background and Problem Statement

Ride-hailing platforms operate as two-sided marketplaces where supply (drivers) and demand (riders) must remain in constant balance. When one side weakens, the entire ecosystem suffers. In Tema, Yango faced a prolonged decline in driver activity that had persisted for nearly three years.

This decline resulted in long estimated time of arrivals (ETAs), reduced ride completion rates, and declining rider confidence. While new driver acquisition campaigns had been attempted in the past, they failed to address a deeper issue: a large pool of registered but inactive drivers who had disengaged from the platform.

Data creates value only when insights are translated into action.

Understanding the Root Cause Through Data

Rather than treating the problem as a simple supply shortage, this project began with a data-driven diagnostic approach. Historical trip data, driver activity logs, and geographic demand patterns were analyzed to understand where and why the system was breaking down.

The analysis revealed several critical insights:

  • High-demand zones consistently lacked active drivers during peak hours
  • A significant percentage of drivers were registered but inactive
  • Long ETAs were discouraging riders, creating a negative feedback loop
  • Previous activation efforts were poorly targeted and location-static
Key Insight:
Demand was not absent — it was misaligned with driver availability.

Designing a Data-Guided Field Strategy

Based on these insights, the project shifted away from generic acquisition tactics toward a targeted, data-guided field strategy. The goal was not just to sign up drivers, but to reactivate dormant ones and restore confidence in the platform.

Drivers were segmented into active, semi-active, and inactive cohorts. Geographic heatmaps were used to identify under-served but high-demand locations, allowing the team to prioritize zones with the highest potential impact.

Execution: From Static Points to Mobile Operations

One of the most important execution decisions was replacing static registration tents with a fully mobile field operation. Instead of waiting for drivers to come to a fixed point, teams moved across identified hotspots—transport terminals, fuel stations, and high-traffic driver zones.

This approach reduced friction, increased visibility, and allowed for real-time engagement with drivers who had previously disengaged due to poor experiences or lack of trust.

Key Insight:
Combining data analysis with field execution produced measurable improvements in driver engagement and ride completion rates.

Results and Measurable Impact

The intervention delivered clear operational improvements within a short timeframe. By aligning supply with demand and rebuilding driver trust, the marketplace began to stabilize.

  • Increased driver reactivation and onboarding rates
  • Reduced ETAs in previously underserved zones
  • Higher ride request acceptance and completion rates
  • Improved rider satisfaction and platform reliability

More importantly, the project demonstrated that sustainable growth requires more than dashboards—it requires execution informed by data.

Key Lessons Learned

This project reinforced several important lessons applicable beyond ride-hailing platforms:

  • Data must inform decisions, not just report outcomes
  • Field execution is most effective when guided by evidence
  • Inactive users often represent untapped value
  • Operational trust is as important as incentives

Conclusion

Reviving ride demand in Tema required more than new sign-ups—it required a strategic alignment of data analysis, business insight, and on-the-ground execution. This case study highlights how data-driven field strategies can transform struggling marketplaces into resilient, growth-ready systems.

When data is treated as a decision-making asset rather than a reporting tool, it becomes a powerful catalyst for operational and business success.
